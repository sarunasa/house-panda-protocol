*House Panda Protocol — Asia-First Venue Strategy*

## Executive Summary

CF Benchmarks (owned by Kraken) has colonized most global crypto ETF pricing, but cracks are opening in Asia. Asian markets are building ETF infrastructure right now with no benchmark providers locked in. HPT's positioning as an Asian-origin institutional token with NVIDIA East Asia cohort backing creates a structural advantage in markets where the competitive set is orders of magnitude smaller than the US.

---

## CF Benchmarks: Current Monopoly Position

CF Benchmarks administers reference rates for all US spot Bitcoin and Ether ETFs (BlackRock, Fidelity, Grayscale, etc.) and expanded to cover 44 digital assets including Aptos, Bittensor, Hedera. System designed to absorb tokens that already have institutional demand — demand comes first, benchmark follows.

**Constituent Exchanges:** Coinbase, Kraken, Bitstamp, LMAX, Crypto.com, Bullish, itBit. Methodology uses equal-weighted volume-weighted medians across these venues.

## Market-by-Market Analysis

### Hong Kong: CF Benchmarks Won, But HKEX Building Challenger

**Current state:** All HK Bitcoin/Ether ETF issuers use CF Benchmarks' Asia Pacific variants (BRRAP, ETHUSD_AP), calibrated to 4pm HKT.

**HKEX Alternative (launched Nov 15, 2024):**
- Virtual Asset Index Series
- Volume-weighted 24-hour spot price, calculated real-time
- Reference rate at 4pm HKT for financial product settlement
- Administered by CCData (CF Benchmarks' main competitor)
- First crypto index compliant with EU BMR developed in Hong Kong

**SFC-licensed exchanges (where HPT needs to be):** HashKey Exchange, OSL

### South Korea: Infrastructure Doesn't Exist Yet

2026 Economic Growth Strategy includes spot Bitcoin ETFs. Korea Exchange chairman announced plans for crypto ETFs and 24/7 trading.

**Critical gap:** Korea has no benchmark provider because they don't have the product yet. "Korea Discount" narrative makes domestic/Asian benchmark politically attractive.

**Key exchanges:** Upbit (~80% volume), Bithumb

### Japan: Targeting 2028

FSA planning overhaul for first spot crypto ETFs by 2028. 2026 tax reform cuts crypto gains to flat 20%. Nomura and SBI preparing ETF products. ¥5 trillion target.

**Key exchanges:** bitFlyer, Coincheck, bitbank

### Singapore: No Retail Crypto ETFs (Deliberate)

MAS routes demand through existing US/HK products. Singapore's play is infrastructure, not consumer-facing ETFs.
