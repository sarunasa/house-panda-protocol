# Securities Law & ETF Readiness Checklist

*Howey Test Avoidance and ETF Prerequisites*

---

## Critical Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Marketing language implying returns** — Frame HPT as functional (powers MM borrowing, pays inference), never as "buy and it goes up"
2. **Revenue sharing to holders** — Income flows to Inference Bank and operations, NOT as dividends
3. **Insufficient utility at launch** — Real functional utility from day one
4. **Too much centralization** — Bonding curve immutability is strongest defense
5. **Presale looking like fundraise** — Current position (< 1% to < 5 accredited, 2018) is good

---

## ETF Prerequisites

| Requirement | Notes |
|-------------|-------|
| Market cap + liquidity | Sustained daily volume in hundreds of millions |
| Major exchange listings | Coinbase, Kraken, Binance minimum |
| Institutional custody | Coinbase Custody, Fireblocks, BitGo |
| Not a security | Formal legal opinion required |
| Decentralization narrative | Bonding curve immutability helps |
| ETF sponsor interest | Bitwise, 21Shares, VanEck must believe in category |

---

## Technical Requirements

- ERC-20 or SPL token standard (custody-compatible)
- No transfer taxes or on-chain fees (NAV calculation)
- Bonding curve as separate contract (not in token transfer)
- Clean cap table documentation from day one
- CME reference rate path (sustained regulated volume)

---

## Strongest Defense

Immutable bonding curve = smart contract-enforced rules team cannot change. Removes "efforts of others" argument. "No governance vote, the curve decides."
